Build Schools, End Poverty

  • With your help we can bring lasting change to children's lives by giving them an education. BASIK provides schools with running water, desks, toilets and a building that will last. We also ensure that children who cannot afford to go to school can go and give all pupils a malaria net to take home.

    We are working to end poverty through education and give children the opportunity to have a brighter future.

    Get in touch

  • Our first project, Kilimani

    BASIK began life in 2006 when we started raising money to fund the rebuilding of Kilimani Primary School.

    The school was a mud hut in a rural location outside Mombasa, with two teachers supporting 70 students. It had no basic facilities and materials. We worked with Head Teacher Julius Nzai to rebuild the school and support children who couldn't afford to pay their fees to get an education.

    Kilimani now has brick built classrooms for all year groups, a staff room, toilets, clean running water and more teachers to provide a better quality education. The school now supports over 150 students from the local community! This was our first project as a charity and we're very proud of what we helped to achieve at Kilimani and the opportunities the students will gain from an education.

  • Our current project

    We are now working in partnership with Simon Musila and the Tsavo East Community Development Initiative. TECDI are a community organisation in Kenya that like us are hoping to educate children as a long-term solution to fight poverty. Simon runs the initiative in his spare time to try and help his community. They have started a community library and museum as well as coming together to support children with their school fees. They do this around the Tsavo East National Park in the Kasaala disctrict.

    If you would like to find out more or get involved with the charity, please email Chris or Celia.
