Build Schools, End Poverty

  • With your help we can bring lasting change to children's lives by giving them an education. BASIK provides schools with running water, desks, toilets and a building that will last. We also ensure that children who cannot afford to go to school can go and give all pupils a malaria net to take home.

    We are working to end poverty through education and give children the opportunity to have a brighter future.

    Get in touch

  • What we do

    BASIK was setup in 2006 to help fund education in Kenya. All money raised by the charity is used to provide schools with necessities such as running water, sanitation blocks, desks and a building that lasts.

    The majority of children in Kenya are coming to schools in very poor conditions. There are no school meals, drinking water, toilets and some schools are falling into disrepair.

  • Where we do it

    BASIK is currently building Kilimani School, near Mombassa, Kenya. We arrived at Kilimani in 2006 and adopted the school as BASIK's first project.

    The school was a mud hut in a remote area in the middle of several villages. One man and a female helper were trying to teach 80 local children of primary school age on a site with no water or sanitary facilities and no teaching resources. In the rainy season, it was impossible to use the hut due to the leaking roof and mud floor.

  • Who we are

    Based in Ilkley, West Yorkshire, BASIK is a family run charity that relies on the dedication and support of friends and volunteers. We organise regional fundraising events, including an annual cycling challenge.

    If you would like to find out more or get involved with the charity, please email Chris or Celia.
